# Objection Handling & Reply Cheat Sheet
**Use for:** Responding to cold outreach replies from e-commerce store owners
**Tone:** Confident, helpful, no pressure — lead with clarity not pushback

---

## OBJECTION 1: "How long does this actually take?"

**What they're really asking:** Will this disrupt my store? Is this a long process?

**Reply:**
> Hi [Name], great question — the whole process on your end takes about 10 minutes total. You export your product CSV from Shopify (takes 2 minutes), send it to me, and I handle everything from there. You get the fully optimized CSV back within 48 hours, then upload it in one click. That's it — no dev work, no downtime, no disruption to your live store.

**Key point to hit:** 48 hours turnaround, 10 minutes of their time total.

---

## OBJECTION 2: "Does this work with Shopify? / What platforms do you support?"

**What they're really asking:** Is this going to break something or not work with my setup?

**Reply:**
> Yes — we deliver everything as a standard CSV that imports directly into Shopify using their built-in product importer. You just check "overwrite existing products" and upload. It works exactly the same way for WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and Magento. No plugins, no integrations, no developer needed. Just a clean CSV import — the same process you'd use to update any product data.

**Key point to hit:** Standard CSV, native importer, zero technical risk.

## OBJECTION 3: "Can you guarantee I'll rank on Google?"

**What they're really asking:** Is this worth $497 if it doesn't work?

**Reply:**
> Honest answer: no one can guarantee specific Google rankings — not us, not any SEO agency. What I can guarantee is that every listing will be fully optimized to Google's current best practices — proper title length, keyword-targeted meta descriptions, structured HTML descriptions, and alt text. That's the foundation Google needs to rank your products. Most stores we work with see meaningful organic traffic improvements within 4–8 weeks. But the guarantee I stand behind 100% is this: if you're not satisfied with the quality of the output after two rounds of revisions, I'll refund you in full.

**Key point to hit:** Be honest about rankings, emphasize the satisfaction guarantee.

---

## OBJECTION 4: "Is it safe to import a CSV? Will it overwrite or delete my products?"

**What they're really asking:** I'm scared of breaking my store.

**Reply:**
> Completely safe — and here's why. The CSV we deliver only updates the text fields (title, description, meta tags, alt text). It doesn't touch your pricing, inventory, SKUs, images, or variants. When you import with "overwrite existing products" checked in Shopify, it updates only the products that match by handle — nothing gets deleted, nothing gets duplicated. We also strongly recommend keeping a backup of your original CSV before importing (Shopify lets you export it in 2 clicks), just for peace of mind. We've done this for dozens of stores and no one has ever had an import issue.

**Key point to hit:** Only updates text fields, nothing gets deleted, backup is easy.

## OBJECTION 5: "Why $497? That seems expensive."

**What they're really asking:** What's the ROI? Can I justify this?

**Reply:**
> Fair question — here's the math. If even 2–3 of your products start ranking on page 1 of Google for a relevant search term, and each drives 50 extra visitors a month at a 2% conversion rate and $50 average order value — that's $150/month in new revenue from just 3 products. Most catalogs have 20, 50, or 100+ products we optimize. At $497 one-time (no subscription, no monthly fee), you typically break even in the first month if even a small percentage of the optimization converts. Compare that to an SEO agency charging $1,500–$3,000/month for the same outcome.

**Key point to hit:** One-time fee, break-even math, compare to agency cost.

## OBJECTION 6: "I already have someone handling my SEO."

**What they're really asking:** Why do I need this if I have an agency?

**Reply:**
> That's great — most SEO agencies focus on blog content, backlinks, and technical site SEO, which is important. But product listing optimization is a different discipline — it's about making each individual product page rank for its own specific search terms. A lot of agencies don't touch product-level copy at all. This is a one-time, done-for-you overhaul of your catalog that complements whatever broader SEO strategy you have running. It's not a replacement — it fills the gap most agencies leave.

**Key point to hit:** Complements existing SEO, fills the product-level gap.
